How it works: The system has 4 layers that transform raw marketplace data into profitable listings:

  • Scraping Layer: Multi-strategy scraping tries the fastest approach first (direct API), then falls back through increasingly powerful (but slower/expensive) methods. Most items are scraped via API in <1 second.
  • Intelligence Layer: Every scraped item is identified (brand, model, rarity), priced (market data + fee analysis), and tracked (price history, trends).
  • Decision Layer: Items are scored on 7 axes (profit, velocity, risk, capital efficiency, rarity, competition, data quality), then filtered through portfolio constraints (max 35% in one category, max active listings, max capital).
  • Action Layer: Approved items are listed on eBay with LLM-enriched descriptions, then continuously monitored for stockout. If the source item sells, the eBay listing is auto-delisted within 5-15 minutes.

Why multiple strategies? Different sites have different anti-bot protections. The system adapts:

  • Strategy 1: Internal API Mercari JP, Yahoo JP — These platforms have internal REST APIs used by their own apps. We call them directly. No browser needed. ~500ms per request. This is how we scrape 90%+ of items.
  • Strategy 2: Crawl4AI + Camoufox When API is blocked — Camoufox is a Firefox browser modified at the source code level to avoid bot detection. Combined with Crawl4AI's LLM extraction, it can handle any JS-rendered page.
  • Strategy 3: Cloudflare Bypass CF-protected sites (Etsy, some others) — A Docker service that solves Cloudflare challenges using a real browser, caches clearance cookies for hours, and provides a simple HTTP API.
  • Strategy 4: Scrapfly Last resort — Paid API with 98%+ bypass rate across all anti-bot vendors. Costs ~$0.002/request. Used only when everything else fails.

Why product ID matters: To price an item correctly, we need to know exactly what it is. A "camera" could be a $50 point-and-shoot or a $2000 Leica. The identifier extracts:

  • Brand: Matched against 200+ known brands across 6 categories. Multi-word brands matched first (e.g., "Comme des Garçons" before "Garçons").
  • Model/MPN: Regex patterns extract manufacturer part numbers, model codes, UPC/EAN barcodes. "DMC-GH5" → Panasonic GH5. "α7 IV" → Sony A7 IV.
  • Category: Keyword-based classification into 10 categories. Source category (Mercari's hierarchy) adds bonus weight.
  • Rarity: Detects "limited edition", "first press", "discontinued", "vintage" signals. A rare item gets a +30% pricing premium.
  • Condition: Normalizes Japanese/English terms: 新品 → new, 美品 → like_new, 中古 → good.

The pricing engine doesn't just compute margin — it optimizes for capital efficiency. Here's the full fee stack:

Source Costs (Mercari JP)

• Item price (converted JPY→USD)
• Mercari selling fee: 10%
• Payment processing: 3%

Proxy Service (Buyee/ZenMarket)

• Receiving fee: ¥200-400
• Consolidation: ¥500
• Payment fee: 3%

Shipping (Japan → US)

• ePacket (0.5kg): $15
• EMS (0.5kg): $22
• DHL Express (0.5kg): $38
• Insurance: 2% of value

Target Fees (eBay)

• Final value fee: 12-15% (by category)
• Payment processing: 2.9% + $0.30
• Promoted listing: 2%+ (optional)

Dynamic pricing strategy:

  • Base price = 75th percentile of eBay sold listings (competitive but profitable)
  • Condition adjustment: New +15%, Good +0%, Fair -15%, Poor -30%
  • Rarity premium: Limited/vintage items +30%
  • Competition positioning: If price is below the "competition zone", position 20% above it
  • Psychological pricing: $49.99, $199.99, $599.99

Key metric: Profit per day — A $30 profit item that sells in 3 days ($10/day) beats a $200 profit item that takes 30 days ($6.67/day). Capital turnover is everything.

Why 7 axes? Profit alone is misleading. A $200 profit item that takes 60 days to sell, has 20% stockout risk, and ties up $150 capital is worse than a $40 profit item that sells in 3 days with 2% risk on $30 capital.

📊 Profit Score (25-35%)

Absolute profit ($) + margin (%). $25+ profit with 30%+ margin = high score. Weighted higher for vintage cameras (patient buyers).

⚡ Velocity Score (10-30%)

How fast it sells. 3 days = 100pts, 7 days = 85pts, 30 days = 25pts. Weighted highest for trading cards (fast turnover critical).

🛡️ Risk Score (10-20%)

Stockout probability, price volatility, counterfeit risk, data confidence. Lower risk = higher score.

💰 Capital Efficiency (15-25%)

Profit per day per dollar deployed. $0.50+/day/$ = excellent. Weighted highest for streetwear (trends change fast).

Portfolio constraints prevent over-concentration:

  • Max 35% of capital in any single category
  • Max active listings limit (default 100)
  • Max capital deployed limit (default $5,000)
  • Category capacity: if we have 10+ active listings in one category, new candidates are deprioritized

The capital optimizer treats this as a portfolio problem. Given fixed capital, how do we allocate across categories to maximize total daily profit?

Example: $2,000 Capital

Trading Cards: 35 listings × $25 = $875
→ 50% margin, 3 days, 85% sell-through
→ $1,275/mo

Anime Figures: 10 listings × $50 = $500
→ 35% margin, 7 days, 75% sell-through
→ $450/mo

Vintage Cameras: 3 listings × $120 = $360
→ 30% margin, 14 days, 60% sell-through
→ $135/mo

Key Insight

Trading cards generate the most profit per dollar because of high velocity (3-day turnover) and high sell-through (85%). Even though per-item profit is low ($12.50), the capital rotates 10x per month.

Vintage cameras have high per-item profit ($36) but slow turnover (14 days) and lower sell-through (60%). Capital is tied up longer.

The optimizer automatically shifts capital toward the most efficient categories based on real performance data.

7. Complete Pipeline — Step by Step

Step 1: Discovery Scan

Search Mercari JP for a category (e.g., "anime figure rare"). The multi-strategy scraper tries the internal API first (~500ms/page), falls back to Crawl4AI+Camoufox if blocked. Returns 20-50 candidate items per scan.

Step 2: Product Identification

Each item is analyzed: brand extracted from 200+ known brands, model/MPN extracted via regex, category classified via keyword matching, rarity scored (limited/vintage/discontinued signals), condition normalized (Japanese→English).

Step 3: Market Price Lookup

For each item, search eBay sold listings via the Finding API (free). Collect 30-50 sold prices. Compute median, percentiles, trend (rising/falling/stable). This gives us the market value.

Step 4: Profit Analysis

Full fee calculation: source fees (Mercari 10% + 3%), proxy fees (Buyee ¥700), shipping (ePacket/EMS/DHL by weight), eBay fees (12-15% + 2.9% + $0.30), insurance, LLM costs. Net profit = sale price - source cost - all fees.

Step 5: Dynamic Pricing

Optimal listing price = 75th percentile of sold prices, adjusted for condition, rarity, competition position, and psychological pricing. Target: price that sells within the category's average days-to-sell.

Step 6: Composite Scoring

Score each item on 7 axes (profit, velocity, risk, capital efficiency, rarity, competition, data quality). Weights vary by category. Items below threshold are rejected with specific reasons.

Step 7: Portfolio Filtering

Apply portfolio constraints: max 35% in one category, max active listings, max capital. Items that would over-concentrate the portfolio are deprioritized. Remaining items are ranked by composite score.

Step 8: Capital Allocation

The capital optimizer generates an optimal allocation plan across categories. Rebalance actions tell us which categories to increase/decrease. This feeds back into Step 7's constraints.

Step 9: Listing

Top-ranked items are enriched by the LLM (Japanese→English translation, compelling description, legal caveats, item specifics). Images are processed (resized, watermarked removed). Listed on eBay via the Sell API at the optimal price.

Step 10: Inventory Sync

A polling loop checks every active source listing every 5-15 minutes. If the source item shows "sold", the eBay listing is immediately ended via the API. Telegram alerts notify you of delists. Target: <3% failure rate.

Step 11: Market Learning

Every sale, every price check, every delist is recorded. The market tracker builds increasingly accurate price histories. The capital optimizer uses real performance data to improve allocation. The system gets smarter over time.

The decision tree has 3 outcomes:

  • 🟢 STRONG BUY (score ≥ 75): List immediately. High profit, fast turnover, low risk. These are the gems — rare items in high-demand categories with strong margins.
  • 🟡 BUY (score 55-74): List when ready. Solid items that meet all thresholds but aren't exceptional. The bulk of your listings will be here.
  • 🟠 HOLD (score 40-54): Marginal — review manually. Might be worth listing if you have spare capacity, but not auto-approved.
  • ❌ SKIP (score < 40 or hard fail): Don't list. Reasons are specific: "Net profit $8 < $15 minimum", "Days to sell 45 > 30 max", "Stockout risk 22% > 15% max".
